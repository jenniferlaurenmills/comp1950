Introduction

The ZeroCoolers are an eclectic bunch. Some of us are nocturnal, some are diurnal, while some are only awake during weekends. But we all share a burning love to create the bestest websites ever. And as students of COMP 1950, we get our five minutes of fame to propose a revamp of the current course website to make it more accessible and informative for everyone. This site will act like a "live" textbook / resources for the current COMP 1950 students, the main target audience. Based on the questionnaire answers, we have a few design goals we will attempt to address:

  • A more intuitive way to access information:
    • visual cues such as: colors schemes, layout, and text, etc.
    • search function incorporated into the site
    • some form of CMS to help the instructor keep the content up to date while refraining from FTP
    • keep users informed of the latest updates through a blog
    • ensuring cross-platform, cross-browser, readability
    • a wiki that provides an evolving collective knowledge base
    • In keeping with this goal, at the very least every page must have:
      • access to previous lectures/exercises/assignments
      • general course outline
      • instructor information
      • access to the course blog
  • The site should also act as a way for prospective students to get an idea of how the course will be taught and what they expect to learn from it.
  • The possibility of an alumni (COMP 1950 alumni) area, sometimes grad students can provide some learning tips as well as some inspiring real world post-grad experience
  • Perhaps incorporate some humor to make a more or less technical / mundane subject more interesting and "digestible" to students
